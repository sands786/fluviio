01 — 03 · The Protocol

Real streaming. Not a simulation.

Fluvio is Initia's native money streaming protocol — the first protocol where "real-time" actually means real-time. Instead of sending 1,000 INIT in a single lump transaction, you deposit into a Fluvio vault and define a flow rate. The contract streams 0.000012 INIT to the recipient every 100 milliseconds — the exact interval of Initia's block time.

The recipient's claimable balance grows every single block. They can withdraw at any moment — mid-stream, without cancelling. The sender can cancel at any time, and unstreamed INIT returns to their vault instantly.

This is money streaming as infrastructure, not as a UI trick.

02 — 03 · The Advantage

Superfluid fakes it. We don't.

Superfluid on Ethereum is the market leader in money streaming, with over $500M TVL. But it has a dirty secret: it doesn't actually stream. Superfluid calculates your "real-time balance" client-side using JavaScript: lastSettled + (flowRate × secondsElapsed). The Ethereum blockchain sees nothing between 12-second blocks. The streaming is an illusion rendered in your browser.

On Fluvio, the Move function timestamp::now_milliseconds() returns a value that actually changes every 100ms — because Initia produces a block every 100ms. When you call claimable_amount() on our contract, it returns a number calculated from real on-chain timestamps, not browser math. The state is real. The blockchain knows about every millisecond.

This matters for three reasons: micro-streams become economically viable (no $5 gas per tick), liquidation detection is near-instant instead of needing sentinel bots, and composability is real — any Initia contract can read stream state trustlessly.

03 — 03 · The Architecture

Three contracts. One ecosystem primitive.

Fluvio is three Move contracts deployed on Initia testnet at address init17g5nnyjfkhnjg4w2m82m9st6lxdhuw62zjgsmd.

stream_vault.move handles escrow. Users deposit INIT into their personal vault. The vault tracks total balance and how much is locked for active streams. You cannot withdraw locked funds — the math is enforced on-chain.

stream_core.move is the heart of the protocol. It stores every stream as a struct with sender, recipient, rate_per_ms, start_time_ms, and end_time_ms. The claimable_amount() function uses timestamp::now_milliseconds() — Initia's 100ms-resolution clock — to calculate exactly how much has streamed since the last withdrawal. Pure math, no oracle.

stream_registry.move is a public oracle. Any contract or app on Initia can call get_global_flow_rate(), get_active_stream_count(), and get_all_time_value_streamed(). Fluvio is not just an app — it is infrastructure the entire Initia ecosystem can build on.

Use Cases

Real-time Payroll

Replace monthly salary runs with continuous streams. Employees and contractors receive INIT into their .init wallet every millisecond they work. No more waiting for payday — your balance grows as you work. For DAOs and remote-first teams, this is the most equitable and transparent payroll system ever built.

Subscriptions

Stream subscription fees instead of charging monthly lump sums. A $10/month newsletter becomes 0.000000038 INIT per millisecond. Subscribers can cancel the stream at any moment and the unstreamed portion returns to their vault instantly. No chargebacks. No disputes. No waiting for refunds.

Infrastructure Rental

Pay for servers, APIs, storage, and compute by the millisecond. The moment you stop your stream, payment stops. No monthly invoices. No overpaying for idle resources. For developers building on Initia, this enables a genuinely usage-based infrastructure economy.

Protocol Grants

Stream grants to developers building in the Initia ecosystem. Instead of large milestone payouts that create perverse incentives, grant funds flow continuously — aligned with ongoing contribution. Initia Labs could stream INIT to Fluvio's team in real time right now, using our own protocol.

Gaming Rewards

Every second a player spends in a game, INIT flows into their .init wallet. True play-to-earn — not a promise, not a token unlock schedule, but a live stream of value. Game developers set the flow rate. Players see their balance tick in real time. This is the Web3 gaming revenue model reimagined.

Cross-chain Streaming

Deposit USDC from Ethereum via Interwoven Bridge. It arrives as INIT in your Fluvio vault. Start streaming immediately to any .init address on Initia. One flow across multiple chains, zero friction. This is the cross-chain UX Web3 has promised for years — Fluvio and Initia deliver it.

How It Works

01

Connect Your .init Wallet

Open Fluvio and connect with Keplr. Initia's .init username system means your streaming identity is human-readable — shahmeer.init, not 0x742d35Cc6634C0532925a3b8D4C9C2D4. Every stream shows real names, not hex strings. This is what onboarding to Web3 should feel like.

02

Deposit INIT to Your Vault

Deposit INIT from your connected wallet into your personal Fluvio vault. Coming from another chain? Use the Interwoven Bridge directly in the app — deposit USDC from Ethereum and it arrives as INIT ready to stream. Your vault tracks your total balance and how much is locked in active streams.

03

Create a Stream

Click "New Stream." Enter the recipient's .init username, the total amount, and the duration. Fluvio calculates the exact rate per millisecond. Approve once with a session key — this is Initia's auto-signing UX — and the stream begins. You will never see another wallet popup for this stream.

04

Watch Money Flow

Your dashboard shows every incoming and outgoing stream with a live counter ticking every 100ms. Recipients can withdraw their claimable balance at any time without cancelling the stream. You can cancel any stream at any time — unstreamed INIT returns to your vault in the same transaction.
